Chapter 5 – Research Writing

Core concepts, structure and best practices for academic research writing

Overview

During this lecture we focused on four main parts of research writing and the key concepts you need to plan, write and present a clear academic paper.

Four Main Parts of Research Writing

  1. Research Process — Systematic & structured methods used to conduct academic research: reviewing existing work, collecting data, analysing results and drawing conclusions.
  2. Paper Structure — The essential components of a research paper/report (title, abstract, introduction, literature review, methodology, results, discussion, conclusion, references).
  3. Citation Methods — Ways to credit sources and avoid plagiarism. Common styles: APA (American Psychological Association), MLA (Modern Language Association), and Chicago.
  4. Academic Tone — Formal, objective, precise writing. Avoid slang and personal opinion unless explicitly asked for reflective sections.

Key Research Concepts

Research Proposal

A document that outlines the proposed research — a blueprint covering planning, research questions, literature review, and chosen methodology.

Data Analysis

The process of inspecting, cleaning, transforming and modelling data to extract meaningful insights. Use appropriate statistical or computational methods to support conclusions.
