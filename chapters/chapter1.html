Chapter 1 – CV Writing

Learn how to craft a professional CV that stands out.

Introduction

A CV is a formal document providing a detailed summary of a person's abilities, skills, and professional experience for potential employers.

  • CV (Curriculum Vitae) is longer, more detailed, often used for academic or research positions.
  • Resume is shorter, 1–2 pages summarizing skills and experience.
  • CVs and resumes are used differently in countries like the U.S., but often interchangeably in India, Australia, and South Africa.

First Impression

Recruiters are attracted to a clean, professional CV design. For an easy start, platforms like Canva offer pre-built CV templates that you can customize and edit.

Researching a Company

Researching a company helps tailor your application and make a strong impression.

  • Find Keywords: Use terms from their website/job description to get your CV past automated systems.
  • Check for a Photo: Know if a photo is expected in that country. In the U.S. and U.K., it's best to leave it off to avoid bias.
  • Tailor Your Application: Show how your skills and experience match the company's needs.

CV Formatting & Structure

  • Tailor it for the job: Put most relevant skills and experience front and center.
  • Use clear headings: Bold headings for each section to help recruiters quickly find information.
  • Use bullet points: Makes your accomplishments and responsibilities easy to scan and digest.
  • Keep it consistent: Use the same font, font size, and spacing throughout the document.
