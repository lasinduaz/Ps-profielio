Chapter 1 – CV Writing

Learn how to craft a professional CV that stands out.

Introduction

A CV is a formal document that provides a detailed summary of a person's abilities, skills, and professional experience for a potential employer.

  • A CV (Curriculum Vitae) is longer and detailed, often for academic/research positions.
  • A resume is shorter, 1–2 pages summarizing skills and experience.
  • CVs and resumes are sometimes used interchangeably depending on the country.

Researching a Company

Researching a company helps you tailor your application and make a strong impression.

  • Find Keywords: Use keywords from their website/job description to pass automated systems.
  • Check for a Photo: Know if a photo is expected; in U.S./U.K., best to leave off to avoid bias.
  • Tailor Your Application: Show how your skills perfectly match the company's needs.

CV Formatting & Structure

  • Tailor it for the job: Put your most relevant skills front and center.
  • Use clear headings: Bold headings for each section.
  • Use bullet points: Easy to scan and digest information.
  • Keep it consistent: Same font, size, and spacing throughout.
