Chapter 8 – Email Writing

Learn the essentials of professional email communication

What is Email?

An email is a digital messaging system used to send messages over the internet from one person to another. Through email, you can send text, images, links, and documents. It can be used for professional, academic, and personal communication. Gmail is one of the most popular email providers, offered by Google.

Structure of an Email

A professional email follows a clear structure to ensure your message is easy to read. Key components include:

  • Subject Line: A brief, clear summary of the email's purpose.
  • Salutation: A polite greeting for the recipient.
  • Body: The main message, which can include multiple paragraphs.
  • Closing Statement: A polite final sentence before your sign-off.
  • Closing: A professional sign-off (e.g., "Sincerely,").
  • Signature: Your name and contact information.

Common Mistakes

  • Writing everything in the subject line
  • Forgetting a proper greeting
  • Using an informal tone

Tips for Writing an Email

  • Subject Line: Keep it clear and specific. Avoid greetings like "Hello" or "Greetings."
  • Salutation: Use a professional greeting. If the recipient’s name is unknown, use "Dear Hiring Manager" or "Dear Team."
  • Closing: Sign-off professionally with your name and contact info. Examples: "Best regards," "Sincerely."
  • Language: Use formal, polite, and respectful language throughout.
