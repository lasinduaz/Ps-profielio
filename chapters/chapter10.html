Chapter 10 – Professional, Telephone and Dining Etiquette

Understanding workplace etiquette when communicating and dining with colleagues.

Overview

This chapter covers Telephone and Dining Etiquette. These etiquettes are important professional manners when communicating with co-workers and dining in workplace settings where you need to behave respectfully and professionally.

Telephone Etiquette

Using etiquette in telecommunication is essential. When taking calls:

  • Need to speak very clearly with respect.
  • During the call, maintain courteous and professional behavior.
  • Avoid eating while on a call.

Also, when answering calls:

  • Answer as soon as possible.
  • Use professional greetings when addressing people.

Manners when using the telephone:

  • When transferring a call, explain and ask permission before doing so.
  • When returning calls, always verify the person first.

Dining Etiquette

Dining etiquette refers to behaving in a professional manner at the table and when interacting with others. This ensures a respectful dining experience with colleagues.

Some common dining etiquettes:

  1. Salt and Pepper Etiquette: Always pass salt and pepper together.
  2. Napkin Etiquette: Before starting to eat, place the napkin on your lap after the host. Keep it neatly and use it to maintain a clean surface.

Drinking Soup

  • Dip the spoon away from yourself.
  • Sip quietly from the side of the spoon.
  • Do not blow on hot soup to cool it — this is considered impolite.

Cutlery Placement Signals

  • Finished: Fork and knife together at 4 o’clock.
  • Pause: Knife and fork crossed.
  • Never place used cutlery back on the table.
