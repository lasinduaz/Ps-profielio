Chapter 7 – Interview Skills

Best practices for preparing and succeeding in job interviews

Introduction

A job interview is a formal conversation between a job applicant and a company representative. It’s a two-way discussion where the company assesses your suitability, and you learn more about the role.

Interview Strategy

Best strategy is preparing answers for these key questions:

  • What do I want the interviewer to know about myself?
  • What disadvantages or weaknesses do I want to minimize?
  • What do I need to know about this job or this organization?

Effective Communication

Communication is more than words: attitude, style, and tone matter. Mastering these shows confidence and professionalism.

  • Non-verbal communication
  • Listening
  • Responding
  • Questioning

Active listening and both verbal & non-verbal cues are key during interviews.

Outfits for Interview

For Men

DO:

  • Casual yet polished: neat outfit, polished shoes, optional blazer
  • Business Professional: tailored suit, crisp shirt, tie

DON'T:

  • Messy or untucked clothes
  • Poorly fitting outfits
  • Overly casual attire

For Women

DO:

  • Casual but polished outfit or business suit
  • Freshly pressed clothes with well-fitted attire

DON'T:

  • Untucked, messy clothes or poorly fitting suit
  • Mismatched shoes or socks
  • Messy hair or overly casual look

Tips & 6 C’s of Communication

  • Pay attention to the interviewer
  • Show that you are listening
  • Seek clarification if you don’t understand
  • 6 C’s of Effective Communication:
    • Clear
    • Concise
    • Correct
    • Complete
    • Concrete
    • Courteous
  • Other skills: Language support, describing strengths, weaknesses, and work experience
